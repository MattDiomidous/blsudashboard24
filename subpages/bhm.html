Black History Month

Black History Month celebrates the cultural heritage, triumphs, and adversities that are indelible in our country's history, honoring all black people from all periods of U.S. History. We celebrate BHM during February because of two American leaders, Abraham Lincoln and Frederick Douglass, whose birthdays were on the 12th and 14th. President Abraham Lincoln and abolitionist Frederick Douglass stood as the two most influential figures in the national debate over slavery and the future of African Americans. Recently, Black History Month was recognized in countries other than America such as Canada, Ireland, and the United Kingdom.
